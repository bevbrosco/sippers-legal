Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 9, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Sippers ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and discloses information when you use the Sippers mobile application (the "Service"), and explains your privacy rights and how the law protects you.

By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

Definitions

  • Account means a unique account created for you to access the Service.
  • Application or Service refers to Sippers, the mobile software provided by the Company.
  • Company (referred to as "the Company," "We," "Us," or "Our") refers to Sippers, operating from Massachusetts, United States.
  • Personal Data means any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual.
  • Service Provider means a third party that processes data on our behalf.
  • You means the individual using the Service.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

When you create an account and use Sippers, you provide:

  • Email address — used for authentication via one-time passcode (OTP), Google Sign-In, or Sign in with Apple.
  • Username and display name — public identifiers shown on your profile and reviews.
  • Name (optional) — used to personalize your profile if you choose to provide one. May be displayed to other users.
  • Date of birth — used to verify you meet the minimum age requirement (17+). Stored but never displayed publicly.
  • Profile avatar (optional) — image you upload for your profile.
  • Reviews and ratings — written reviews, numerical ratings, and review photos you create.
  • Social interactions — follows, follow requests, and blocks.

Information Collected from Your Device

With your permission, the Service may access:

  • Camera — to capture photos for reviews and your profile.
  • Photo library — to select photos for reviews and your profile.

You can enable or disable these permissions at any time in your device settings.

Crash and Diagnostic Data

To diagnose and fix bugs, we use Sentry to collect crash reports and error logs. This may include device type, operating system version, app version, error stack traces, and a non-personal user identifier. We do not use Sentry or any other tool for behavioral analytics or marketing.

How We Use Your Information

  • Provide and operate the Service — authenticate you, manage your account, display your reviews and profile, and enable social features.
  • Verify your eligibility — confirm that you meet the minimum age requirement (17+).
  • Communicate with you — send authentication codes, account-related security notices, and respond to your support requests. We do not send marketing emails.
  • Diagnose technical issues — investigate crashes and errors to improve reliability.
  • Enforce our Terms — review reports, enforce content rules, and protect against abuse.
  • Comply with legal obligations — respond to lawful requests from authorities.

What We Do Not Do

  • We do not sell your personal information.
  • We do not use behavioral or marketing analytics.
  • We do not show advertisements or share your data with ad networks.
  • We do not use cookies or web tracking (Sippers is a native mobile app).
  • We do not send marketing emails, newsletters, or promotional messages.
  • We do not use your personal information or content to train artificial intelligence or machine learning models, whether ours or any third party's.

Third-Party Service Providers

We use the following third-party services to operate Sippers. Each is bound by its own privacy policy, linked below:

  • Supabase — hosts our database, authentication, and file storage. Receives all account, content, and image data. Privacy Policy
  • Sentry — collects crash and error reports. Privacy Policy
  • Google — provides Google Sign-In as an authentication option. Privacy Policy
  • Apple — provides Sign in with Apple as an authentication option. Privacy Policy
  • Open Food Facts — public database we query for product barcode lookups. We send only product barcodes; no personal data is sent.

How We Share Your Information

  • Public profile and content — your username, display name, avatar, reviews, and ratings are visible to other users of the Service. If you set your account to private, your profile and content are visible only to your approved followers. Direct identifiers like your email address and date of birth are never public.
  • Service Providers — we share data with the providers listed above as needed to operate the Service.
  • Legal compliance — we may disclose information if required by law, court order, or valid government request, or to protect rights, property, safety, or to prevent fraud or abuse.
  • Business transfers — if we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred. We will notify you and ensure protections continue.
  • With your consent — for any other purpose with your explicit consent.

Sharing to External Platforms

Sippers includes a feature that lets you share images of your profile or beverage activity to other apps and services (such as Instagram, Messages, or social platforms) through your device's share menu. The image you create may include your username, display name, profile photo, and beverage statistics. Sharing is initiated entirely by you; we do not transmit data to external services automatically. Once you share content to another platform, it is governed by that platform's own policies.

Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account from within the app (Settings → Delete Account), we permanently remove your account, profile, reviews, and uploaded photos from our active systems.

Reviews that have been reported and are subject to ongoing moderation may be retained as moderation evidence even after a delete request, in accordance with our enforcement responsibilities. Crash and error reports collected by Sentry are retained per Sentry's standard retention policy. Backup copies may persist in encrypted backups for a limited period for disaster recovery.

We may retain certain information longer where required by legal obligation, to defend legal claims, or to address security or fraud issues.

We may also create aggregated, de-identified, or anonymized data from personal information by removing or modifying details that identify you. Such data is not personal information, and we may use, retain, and disclose it for any lawful purpose, including for analytics, research, and improving the Service. We do not attempt to re-identify aggregated or anonymized data.

Your Privacy Rights

Regardless of where you live, you have the following rights with respect to your personal information:

  • Right to access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to correct — update inaccurate or incomplete information. Most fields can be edited directly in the app.
  • Right to delete — request deletion of your account and personal information. You can do this directly in the app at Settings → Delete Account.
  • Right to portability — request a copy of your data in a portable format.
  • Right to object or restrict — object to or restrict certain processing.
  • Right to withdraw consent — withdraw any consent you have previously provided.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at bevbrosco@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

For Users in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Switzerland (GDPR)

If you are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent laws. Our legal bases for processing your personal information are:

  • Contractual necessity — to provide the Service you have requested (account creation, authentication, displaying your reviews).
  • Legitimate interests — to ensure the security and reliability of the Service, prevent abuse, and diagnose technical issues.
  • Legal obligation — to comply with applicable law.
  • Consent — where you have explicitly granted it (for example, camera or photo library access).

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority if you believe we have violated your rights.

For California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act) provides you the following rights:

  • Right to know what personal information we collect, the sources, the business purposes, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.
  • Right to delete personal information we have collected about you.
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. Sippers does not sell or share personal information for advertising purposes.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information — we do not use sensitive personal information for purposes beyond providing the Service.
  • Right to non-discrimination — we will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights.

Categories of personal information we collect (per California's classifications): identifiers (email address, account ID), customer records (username, optional display name), and internet/device activity limited to crash reports. We do not collect commercial information, geolocation, professional information, biometric information, or inferences.

Sources: directly from you, and from authentication providers (Google, Apple) when you choose to sign in with them.

Categories of third parties with whom we share information: service providers listed above (Supabase, Sentry, Google, Apple). We do not share personal information with data brokers, ad networks, or analytics companies.

To exercise your California rights, contact us at bevbrosco@gmail.com. You may designate an authorized agent to make a request on your behalf, in which case we may verify the agent's authority.
